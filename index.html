01 / The problem

The best deals are found before they reach a banker's auction. Small firms are worst equipped to find them.

Lower-mid-market PE returns are made at origination. The moment a deal hits a competitive auction, price and returns compress. Two things make proprietary origination hard — and they are exactly the two things a 10–15 person firm does by hand.

Problem 01

Finding the right targets, reading the moment

Switzerland publishes almost no private-company financials. Just building a clean list of companies that fit the mandate is hard — and knowing which are quietly heading toward a transaction is harder. The signal is buried in registry filings, regional press, and structural moves nobody systematically watches.

Problem 02

Getting to the owner, warmly

Swiss owners engage people they trust — their auditor, their bank, a mutual contact — not whoever cold-called. The relationship wins the deal, and that relationship lives scattered across the firm's inboxes, calendars, and partners' heads.

Today this is done with manual research, personal networks, and instinct. The global software suites don't help: built for US-scale breadth, blind to Swiss private companies, and they pull the firm's data out into a US cloud.

02 / Why it matters

A structural wave of Swiss ownership change, with no map.

~93k
Swiss SME owners facing succession within five years — and most family-internal handovers fail.
~110
Core serious-addressable Swiss accounts doing proprietary buy-side origination, plus a softer ~100-account tail.
5–7 yr
Swiss deal cycle — long enough that the firm needs felt value on day one, not just a closed deal.

There is no list of "who fits, and who's in play." That absence is the opportunity.

03 / The core insight

The behaviour is the product.

The kestrel hovers over a wide field, scans patiently, and strikes on the one movement that matters.

Crivel is that, almost literally: continuously monitor a huge universe of companies, surface the one signal that matters, and find the precise, warmest line in. Not a brute-force dive — the patient scan, then the exact strike.

  • The trusted advisor is a first-class node. In Swiss SME M&A, the statutory auditor (Revisionsstelle) is public in the register. One Treuhand can be the warmest path to forty family-firm doors — and we can build that graph deterministically, for free.
  • Fit doesn't need a sell-signal. A strong-fit company with a warm path is worth pursuing even with no sell-signal at all. Timing raises priority; it never decides eligibility.
  • The firm's own strategy is the filter. The same public event produces a different, prioritized output for every client — because it's ranked against their thesis and their network.

06 / Differentiation & moat

The moat is the fusion, not the model.

The LLM is commoditizing and the open registry data is scrapeable. The defensible position is where the three layers meet — and the fact that it compounds with every analyst correction.

Layer 01

In-tenant by design

The firm's sensitive data stays inside its own cloud boundary, Swiss-resident. Every incumbent pulls data out — keeping it in is both moat and compliance story.

Layer 02

DACH-deep entity graph

A persistent, continuously-enriched Swiss corporate-control graph with owner inference the US suites can't match on small private firms.

Layer 03

Early-trigger monitoring

Event detection on the live register stream, tuned to the precursor combinations that predict a Swiss family firm coming to market.

It becomes the origination operating system: pipeline, target notes, outreach history, and pass / pursue decisions all live here. Leaving means losing the firm's institutional sourcing memory — and every correction sharpens the next result.

08 / Market opportunity

A small, concentrated, high-margin niche — and that smallness is a defense.

The market is too small to be worth attacking for the global suites. We anchor pricing on deal value — one extra proprietary deal dwarfs a decade of subscription — not per-seat.

Switzerland-only · realistic execution~1–3M CHF ARR
Switzerland near-saturation + tail~5M CHF (hard ceiling)
With DACH expansion~10M CHF feasible

Category pricing for comparable tools runs CHF / $ 5k–50k+/yr. DACH expansion is gated by register localization and execution, not market size — and Germany's Bundesanzeiger data is better, making the hardest part (sizing) easier.

The economics

  • High one-time engineering — ingestion, entity resolution, inference, the register parser — then near-zero marginal cost per additional client.
  • The shared substrate is shared across all clients, which is why a 20–50k CHF/yr price stays high-margin.
  • AI runs only at the ends of the funnel, never in the constant middle — cost scales with the handful of targets that matter, not the size of the register.
  • Onboarding is "connect your network, set your thesis." Classic SaaS shape, in a niche small enough to defend and large enough for the business we want.
